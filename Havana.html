Food & Drinks

EIn the 20s and 30s Americans flocked to Havana to enjoy exotic mojitos made with Havana Club rum. In 1960 the Cuban Revolution led to Castro nationalising the distillery but the essence of Cuba can still be found on a tour of the “Museo del Ron”.

Places to see

El Malecon is Havana’s famed esplanade whose glitzy and glamorous past has now become a symbol of the impact of Castro’s restrictive communist regime. The faded and dilapidated hotels and apartments that would have once been home to the rich and famous are now a sobering reality check of a once vibrant and international city.

Things to do

The very essence of Cuban spirit and attitude is epitomised in the colourful and flamboyant salsa displays that are everywhere around the city. Be prepared to be hot, sweaty and move those feet as the latino rhythm gets into your heart and the sound of Cuban salsa takes over your soul.
